# Greedy Algorithms

## Reading Material
- Chapter 6 of Antti Laaksonen's *Competitive Programmer's Handbook* (available online for free [here](https://cses.fi/book/book.pdf))
- Chapter 3.4 of Steven and Felix Halim's *Competitive Programming 2* (available online for free [here](https://www.comp.nus.edu.sg/~stevenha/myteaching/competitive_programming/cp2.pdf))
- Some editorials of your choice from [GeeksForGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/dsa/greedy-algorithms/) to get used to the greedy approach and how to apply it in different problems.
- If you have time, [Jeffe's Greedy Algorithms](https://jeffe.cs.illinois.edu/teaching/algorithms/book/04-greedy.pdf) to get used to the mathematical proof of greedy algorithms, which is a very important skill to have.
- Advanced but more Math-oriented, [Olympiad Combinatorics - Pranav A. Sriram](https://dn710207.ca.archive.org/0/items/olympiad-combinatorics/Olympiad_Combinatorics.pdf) 

*For those who follow the roadmap, come back to the last book in the midde of phase 4 or later.*

*Side-note : reading some editorials will help you grasp the concepts, but solving problems is the key to mastering greedy algorithms.*
